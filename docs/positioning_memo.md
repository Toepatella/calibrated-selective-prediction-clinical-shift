# Positioning memo (applied)

*One-sentence contribution, the scope decision behind it, and the venue fit. The method spine — notation, weights, thresholds, budgets — is the single source of truth in [method_note.md](method_note.md) (§1 notation; §3 shift-aware calibration; §4 OOD routing; §6 the clinician-facing audit layer; §7 assumptions and honest limitations). The evaluation protocol is preregistered in [preregistration.md](preregistration.md).*

This memo states what the paper claims, what it deliberately does **not** claim, and why the honest, measure-and-report stance is the right fit for the target venue. The discipline is the one carried throughout the method note: every guarantee is cited as a property **of the method that owns it, under that method's own assumptions**; we **claim no new guarantee** for the combined pipeline; and where realistic clinical shift breaks a method's assumptions, we **measure and report** the resulting degradation rather than claim it away.

## Contribution (one sentence)

**An applied, trustworthy, auditable selective-prediction pipeline for clinical image classification under realistic covariate + label shift — assembled entirely from existing, citable methods (weighted conformal / RCPS, BBSE and MLLS+BCTS label-shift estimation, post-hoc OOD routing) — whose contribution is a measurement / auditability discipline and a clinician-facing trust interface that make the residual shift-induced degradation visible at the point of care, NOT a new statistical guarantee.**

The pipeline answers only where it has evidence it can stand behind (`A(x)`, the in-scope/answered event of method_note §1.5.1), abstains on uncertain cases, routes suspected out-of-distribution scans to a human, and corrects calibration for covariate and label shift between a labeled **source** and a deployment **target**. The object we care about is the answered-case target risk `R_T^accept := E_{P_T}[ ℓ(Y, ŷ(X)) | A(X) ]` (method_note §1.1). We tune operating thresholds on calibration data, apply the weighted correction, and then **measure** realized selective risk, coverage, and OOD-leakage on held-out target data — reporting the degradation, overall and by subgroup. We do **not** certify `R_T^accept`.

**Auditability as explanation, not just UQ.** When a clinician asks an AI system "why," two distinct questions hide inside the word. One is about the *world*: why does this scan indicate disease — which features, which biology, which causal pathway. The other is about the *model*: why should I trust this particular automated output, and when the system stays silent, why did it decline. This paper is squarely about the second question, and we argue that answering it faithfully is itself a form of clinical explainability — the form that is most actionable at the point of care and most defensible under distribution shift, where a confident-looking answer is exactly the thing a clinician most needs to be warned about. We call this stance **auditability as explanation** (developed in full in [explainability_framing.md](explainability_framing.md)): an interpretable, per-case, subgroup-stratified account of the model's *own reliability* and of the reason behind every accept / defer / route decision, surfaced where the decision is made (method_note §6). For each answered in-scope case the audit layer exposes the recalibrated posterior `σ̃(f(x))` (so the displayed confidence means what a clinician reads it to mean), a representativeness chip from the combined weight `ŵ(x,ŷ)` (how typical the case is of the data the model was calibrated and audited on), a cohort reliability flag `n_eff`, and a shift-regime badge; for every declined case it names which reliability gate fired — uncertainty (`u(x) > τ̂`), suspected out-of-distribution (`o(x) > t̂_ood`), or untrustworthy covariate weight (`ŵ_cov(x) > w_max`) — together with the margin to each threshold. Because the explanation *is* the decision machinery surfaced rather than a post-hoc reconstruction of a black box, the per-case decline explanation is **routing-faithful** — faithful to the routing decision, not to the diagnosis: the gates and signed margins it reports are the very predicates the pipeline evaluated to accept / abstain / route, not a plausible-looking story approximating them. The displayed reliability numbers (the recalibrated posterior `σ̃(f(x))`, the representativeness chip `ŵ(x,ŷ)`, the `n_eff` flag) are likewise the pipeline's *own* finite-sample estimates and measured diagnostics rather than a surrogate's — but, being estimates, they remain subject to the measured shift-induced degradation the audit layer exists to surface, never a guarantee that the displayed confidence or the prediction is correct. This maps directly onto the collection's stated targets — "explainability techniques designed for clinical auditability" and "explainable decision pathways" that "facilitate responsible deployment." It deliberately does **not** address the collection's other emphasis on disease mechanism and causal patient dynamics: our system explains the *model's* reliability and the reason it abstained or routed, **not** the disease biology, the feature-level patient narrative, or the causal dynamics of the patient — and we state that boundary on purpose. Crucially, casting auditability as explanation introduces **no new guarantee**: every number the layer surfaces remains a finite-sample estimate (`x̂`) or a measured diagnostic under its own method's assumptions, and the separately-measured operating budgets `α_acc` and `α_ood` are not recombined into a deployed-pipeline certificate by the act of displaying them.

## What this memo drops (versus the earlier certified framing)

An earlier version of this project framed the headline as a **finite-sample risk certificate** under prevalence shift, with a conditionally-certified extension to combined shift. That framing is **withdrawn in full**. Specifically, this paper does **not**:

- split the claim into a **primary certified** (pure-label-shift) result and a **secondary certified** (combined-shift) result — there is no certified result at either tier;
- invoke **Factorizable Joint Shift (FJS)** as an *identification / certification* apparatus that anchors a scale constant to yield a certified combined weight. We retain only the weaker, honest version: a **factorizable shift premise adopted as a modeling choice** — the *invariant-density-ratio* condition (`p_T(x∣y)/p_S(x∣y)` class-free on overlapping support; Tasche 2022 Thm 4/Cor 4 at `ρ=1`), **strictly stronger** than the FJS `w(x,y)=U(x)·V(y)` sense of He et al. 2022 — for dense imaging shift, not identifiable from unlabeled target data, with the residual **measured** on a small labeled target slice (method_note §3.3, §7.4);
- treat any quantity (e.g. a model-residual `ε_M`) as a **certificate** of the combined-shift correction. The labeled target slice `D_tar^lab` (`m_lab ≈ 50–200`) exists **only** to empirically measure residual degradation and fit a simple scalar correction — never to identify a nuisance parameter or certify the combined case (method_note §1.7, §7.1);
- use the words **"certify" / "certified" / "guarantee"** for the deployed pipeline anywhere. Each cited method's guarantee is named with its assumption (RCPS `(α,δ)` control under exchangeability; weighted-conformal `1−α` marginal coverage under pure covariate shift with the oracle likelihood ratio; BBSE/MLLS consistency under label shift); none of these survives the source-to-target shift as a deployment certificate, and we say so rather than re-asserting it.

Two published impossibility results make this the *only* honest stance, not a concession of weakness (method_note §7.1): **OOD detection is not distribution-free learnable** in the unrestricted setting (Fang et al., NeurIPS 2022), and **finite-sample distribution-free conditional coverage under covariate shift is not attainable** once the density-ratio weight is estimated (Yang, Kuchibhotla & Tchetgen Tchetgen, JRSS-B 2024). A third structural fact compounds them: **combined covariate-and-label shift is not identifiable from unlabeled target covariates alone.** Together these are why the load-bearing deliverable is a *measured residual with a confidence interval*, not a bound.

**What is new here.** Dropping the certificate is a concession about the *guarantee*, not about the *contribution*; the novelty does not live in a theorem. Four prongs are new as a package, each a measurement / reporting artifact rather than a claim that something is bounded or controlled:

1. **The measurement / auditability discipline itself** — composing published methods under strict fold-disjointness with a fixed, pre-registered set of reported reliability diagnostics, so that every clinician-facing number is a finite-sample estimate (`x̂`) or a measured diagnostic carrying its own assumption and its own measured degradation.
2. **The previously-unquantified OOD gap, turned into one number.** Prior trustworthy-prediction pipelines remove OOD inputs but leave the residual far-OOD *leakage* unquantified; we turn it into a single auditable figure — measured on a *stated, swappable* exposure set `O` and read against the named operating budget `α_ood` (method_note §4.3) — rather than asserting the gap away.
3. **The routing-faithful Decline-Attribution Record** (method_note §6), with an *executable* construction-faithfulness invariant: the displayed gates-and-margins are read off the same routing predicate the pipeline evaluated, and a check asserts that equality holds, so the explanation cannot silently drift from the decision it explains.
4. **The pre-registered honest-negative failure-mode catalog** — the regimes where the composed correction is expected to degrade are named and reported *in advance* (per-pair discriminator AUROC, `κ(Ĉ_S)`, `n_eff`, the `q̂_T`-vs-`Ĉ_S p̂_T` check), so a negative result is a planned, legible finding rather than a surprise.

None of the four is a new guarantee: they make residual degradation *visible and checkable*, they do not bound or control it.

## What this memo keeps (the honest scope)

- **Two imaging benchmarks, depth over breadth.** The pipeline is evaluated on realistic cross-site clinical shift only: **CAMELYON17-WILDS** (cross-hospital scanner/stain change) and **CheXpert ↔ MIMIC-CXR** (cross-site acquisition change). These exhibit mixed covariate + label shift plus appearance changes that move `p(x∣y)` — exactly the regime that violates the pure-shift premises of the component methods, and therefore exactly where the measure-and-report discipline earns its keep.

- **The cleanest regime is named as the cleanest *evaluation* regime, not a certified corollary.** Under **pure label shift** the combined weight collapses to `w(x,y) = w_lab(y) = p̂_T(y)/p_S(y)`, the covariate correction is inert, and a **single** untestable premise (anti-causal `p(x∣y)`-invariance) is in play rather than two. This is where the correction needs the fewest assumptions and where we *observe* the lowest measured realized risk — stated as an empirical observation, not a corollary (method_note §3.5, §7.6). The combined covariate+label case is the stress extension, carrying the extra covariate-weight error and the factorizable-shift modeling residual, both **measured** on `D_tar^lab`.

- **`p(x∣y)`-invariance as a testable regime, reframed around measured diagnostics.** The label-shift premise — that disease appearance does not change between sites — is **not realistic across arbitrary hospital pairs** (different scanners, protocols, and case-mix-correlated demographics all violate it to some degree). We treat it as a **testable regime, not a blanket premise**, and surface it through *measured diagnostics* rather than an assumed-true claim:
  - the **per-hospital-pair domain-discriminator AUROC**, showing how close each pair actually is to "no covariate shift";
  - the **BBSE consistency check** comparing `q̂_T` against `Ĉ_S p̂_T`, flagging gross anti-causal / appearance-shift violation;
  - the **BBSE conditioning diagnostic** `κ(Ĉ_S)`, flagging where the label-shift estimate is least trustworthy (worst for rare, clinically important minority classes);
  - the **Kish effective sample size** `n_eff = (Σ ŵ_i)² / Σ ŵ_i²`, flagging low-effective-sample regimes where the weighted estimate is dominated by a few points.

  A **cohort-level shift-regime badge** (method_note §6.3) then states which correction regime is actually in force for each target site — *pure-label-shift* when the discriminator AUROC is near chance, *combined covariate+label* when it shows real covariate shift — so the cleaner label-shift result is **never silently substituted** for the harder combined case. The badge makes the headline a measured, checkable claim rather than an assumed-true one.

## The contribution restated: a discipline and an interface, not a certificate

The novelty is **organizational and methodological**, not a new theorem:

1. **A measurement / auditability discipline.** We compose published methods with strict fold-disjointness (method_note §1.7), self-normalized (Hájek) weighted risk estimation, a variance-adaptive concentration bound, and a fixed set of **reported reliability diagnostics** (`n_eff`, `κ(Ĉ_S)`, the `q̂_T`-vs-`Ĉ_S p̂_T` check, per-pair discriminator AUROC). Crucially, we turn the previously-unquantified OOD gap into **one auditable number**: the residual far-OOD leakage measured on a *stated, swappable* exposure set `O` against a named operating budget `α_ood` (method_note §4.3). `α_acc` and `α_ood` are named, **separately measured** operating budgets, not a proven additive union-bound split.

2. **A clinician-facing trust interface (explainability-via-auditability).** The audit layer (method_note §6) surfaces, per case, the routing decision over `A(x)`, the recalibrated posterior `σ̃(f(x))`, a per-case weight-context chip (`ŵ(x,ŷ)` as a *representativeness* flag), and the cohort reliability flag `n_eff`; and per cohort, a **subgroup / fairness audit** of selective risk, coverage, and routing rates with finite-sample intervals, plus explicit OOD and shift-regime flags. Its explicit job is to make the residual degradation that the method warns about **visible at the point of care**, not to hide it behind a single number.

**The two legs are distinct, not relabeled UQ.** The paper carries a trustworthiness leg and an explainability leg, and they are different artifacts answering different questions — the explanatory leg is not the uncertainty numbers under a new name:

| Trustworthiness (what is measured) | Explainability (what a clinician reads) |
| --- | --- |
| Measured selective risk `R_T^accept` with finite-sample intervals, overall and by subgroup | The per-case **routing-faithful DAR**: which reliability gate fired (`u(x) > τ̂`, `o(x) > t̂_ood`, `ŵ_cov(x) > w_max`) plus the signed margin to each threshold |
| The OOD-leakage budget: residual far-OOD leakage on the stated exposure set `O` against `α_ood` | The accept-side **representativeness / regime account**: the weight chip `ŵ(x,ŷ)` (how typical the case is), the recalibrated posterior `σ̃(f(x))`, the cohort reliability flag `n_eff`, and the shift-regime badge |
| Reliability diagnostics (`κ(Ĉ_S)`, `n_eff`, per-pair discriminator AUROC, the `q̂_T`-vs-`Ĉ_S p̂_T` check) | A legible per-decision narrative of *why this case was accepted, deferred, or routed* — the decision machinery surfaced, not a post-hoc surrogate |

The left column is uncertainty quantification; the right column is the explanatory contribution. Conflating them is exactly the relabeling this memo refuses: the DAR and the accept-side regime account are *load-bearing explanation*, removing them would not change a single risk number but would remove the paper's answer to "why did the model decline, and how far can I trust this accept."

This is the honest counterpart to prior trustworthy-prediction pipelines (e.g. **TRUECAM**) that restore validity by detecting and removing OOD inputs rather than by a guarantee that provably survives in-scope covariate shift, and do not claim a maintained distribution-free guarantee in deployment. We do not improve on that with a stronger guarantee; we **measure and report the residual leakage** against a stated exposure model and route the flagged tail to a human, rather than leaving the gap unquantified.

## Prior art: the near neighbors (conformal prediction for medical-imaging deployment)

The honest comparison is **not** against the 2018–2021 method primitives (RCPS, weighted conformal, BBSE) — those are *components* we cite as owned guarantees — but against the small, fast-growing cluster of papers that apply conformal prediction / risk control to clinical-imaging *deployment under shift*. Four are close enough that omitting them would read as a literature gap, and a fifth (concurrent, 2026) probes exactly our label-shift regime. We state each one's actual claim and what this paper adds; the differentiator is real but it is a **composition-and-auditability** differentiator, never a stronger guarantee.

| Near neighbor | What it actually does | What this paper adds over it |
|---|---|---|
| **Lu, Ahmed, Singh & Kalpathy-Cramer, "Estimating Test Performance for AI Medical Devices under Distribution Shift with Conformal Prediction"** (ICML 2022 PODS Workshop; arXiv:2207.05796) | Estimates the **aggregate** test accuracy of a black-box model on an *unlabeled* target domain under institution/scanner/atlas/hospital shift, directly from output responses, with no distributional assumptions. The closest match to our "measured answered-case error rate." | We measure a **selective (answered-case) risk** behind an abstention gate, not one aggregate number; we add covariate+label **reweighting**, **OOD / out-of-scope routing**, and the per-case **audit layer**. Shared philosophy (measure, don't guarantee; black-box); different object (selective + routed vs. aggregate). |
| **Angelopoulos, Pomerantz, Do, Bates, Bridge, Elton, Lev, González, Jordan & Malik, "Conformal Triage for Medical Imaging AI Deployment"** (medRxiv 2024.02.09.24302543) | Splits patients into **low-risk (high NPV) / high-risk (high PPV) / uncertain** strata with conformal guarantees, post-hoc, transferring to a local population via local conformal calibration. The "three-bucket routing" idea in conformal clothing — by the **same group** whose RCPS / CRC / LTT we build on. | Triage routes on **clinical risk** (a property of the prediction / `y`); our three gates route on **model reliability** (defer-on-uncertainty, route-on-OOD, route-on-untrustworthy-weight). We add explicit covariate+label **importance reweighting** via the `Z(x)` combine, an **OOD / out-of-scope** screen (triage has none), measurement of the residual under **broken exchangeability**, and the **routing-faithful DAR**. Must be cited *because* we reuse their machinery. |
| **Xu, Guo & Wei, "Selective Conformal Risk Control"** (2025; arXiv:2512.12844) | Formalizes **select-then-control** (conformal prediction + selective classification): SCRC-T (exact finite-sample, preserves exchangeability) and SCRC-I (calibration-only, PAC-style). General-purpose, **under exchangeability**. | This is the formal underpinning of our accepted-region risk control — which we cite — but SCRC lives **under exchangeability**; our contribution is taking select-then-control into the **shifted, OOD-contaminated** clinical regime where its guarantee no longer holds and **measuring** the residual instead. We add shift correction, OOD routing, imaging benchmarks, and the audit layer. |
| **Hulsman, Comte, Bertolini, Wiesenthal, Puertas Gallardo & Ceresa, "Conformal Risk Control for Pulmonary Nodule Detection"** (COPA 2025, PMLR 266:445–463; arXiv:2412.20167) | Applies **CRC** to pulmonary-nodule detection: prediction sets trading false positives vs. sensitivity with formal guarantees (91.35% sensitivity @ 2.25 FP/scan). A within-distribution CRC **application** on chest imaging. | Shares the CRC-on-chest-imaging substrate but is **single-site, with no shift correction, no covariate+label reweighting, no OOD routing, no selective-abstention-for-trust, and no audit layer**. We are the shift-aware *selective + routed + audited* pipeline; they are the in-distribution CRC case study. |

**A concurrent 2026 neighbor that corroborates our stance.** Li, Liu, Zhang et al., "A Deployment Audit of Release-Side Risk in Conformal Triage under Prevalence Shift" (arXiv:2605.20956, May 2026) **audits** conformal triage under **prevalence (label) shift** and finds that **marginal coverage is only a weak event-safety floor** — a system can hold 90% coverage while event-class protection degrades — using a monotone logit-shift prevalence correction and disjoint target-label roles (correction-fit / threshold-cal / eval). It uses *neither* importance reweighting *nor* OOD detection. This is **concurrent corroboration**, not competition: it independently validates two of our explicit positions — the **marginal-vs-class-conditional coverage gap under label shift** (our method_note §7.6 (iv) Mondrian limitation) and the **disjoint labeled-slice discipline** (our `D_tar^lab` role-splitting, method_note §1.7) — while our pipeline goes further with *joint* covariate+label correction, OOD routing, and the audit layer.

**The net differentiator, stated honestly.** No single neighbor combines (1) **joint covariate+label reweighting** via the `Z(x)` combine (Lu / Triage / Hulsman / SCRC reweight for *neither* shift; the 2026 audit corrects prevalence only), (2) a **decoupled far-OOD vs. variance double gate** (none does OOD routing at all), (3) the **measure-and-report-under-broken-exchangeability** stance grounded in the two impossibility results (method_note §7.1), and (4) the **routing-faithful Decline-Attribution audit layer**. Each neighbor addresses one axis well — aggregate estimation (Lu), risk-stratified routing (Triage), the formal select-then-control structure (SCRC), a CRC imaging application (Hulsman), a prevalence-shift audit (Li et al.). Our claim is the **composition across all those axes plus the auditability discipline** — and, consistent with the rest of this memo, we do **not** out-guarantee any of them; we compose what they each do singly and make the residual visible.

**A modality note on TRUECAM.** TRUECAM ("Implementing Trust in Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer Diagnosis … in Whole-Slide Images," [arXiv:2501.00053](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.00053)) is **digital-pathology WSI**, not DICOM radiology. That makes it a **same-modality** methodological foil for our **CAMELYON17** arm (also WSI pathology — we already run a TRUECAM-style detect-and-remove baseline there, prereg §4 item 3) and only a **cross-modality analog** for our **CheXpert ↔ MIMIC-CXR** radiology arm; we state the distinction so TRUECAM reads as the right comparator for the pathology arm and an analog (not a head-to-head competitor) for the radiology arm. TRUECAM also spans multiple foundation backbones (UNI, CONCH, Prov-GigaPath, TITAN), which is part of why we add the scoped two-backbone robustness check (prereg §6.6). For the **radiology** framing specifically, **Conformal Triage** (neuroradiology; PPV/NPV-guaranteed risk strata under exchangeability) is the cleaner direct contrast to our covariate+label weight-correction + measured-residual + audit approach.

## Venue fit — Discover Computing collection on Intelligent Medicine (ML + Explainable AI)

The target is the Springer *Discover Computing* collection **"Intelligent Medicine: Machine Learning and Explainable AI for Next-Generation Healthcare"** (open access; deadline 2026-10-05), whose central emphasis is **explainability and trustworthy / auditable ML** for clinical decision support. The paper maps directly onto the collection's themes:

- **Trustworthy / auditable ML.** The whole pipeline is built so that every risk number a clinician sees is either a finite-sample **estimate** (`x̂`) or a **measured** diagnostic, with its assumption and its measured degradation disclosed — auditability is the contribution, not a side note.
- **Uncertainty quantification.** Calibrated selective prediction with risk-controlled abstention (RCPS / conformal risk control as cited methods), recalibrated posteriors, and reported finite-sample confidence intervals on the *measured* selective risk.
- **OOD robustness under distribution shift.** Explicit covariate + label shift correction with measured residuals, plus a post-hoc OOD routing screen whose leakage is audited against a stated exposure set — directly the collection's robustness-in-deployment concern.
- **Explainability via auditability.** Rather than a saliency-map notion of explanation, the contribution is a clinician-facing trust interface that *explains where and why the model declines, how representative each case is, and where the shift correction is least trustworthy* — an interpretable account of the model's own reliability, surfaced at the point of care. Its named explanatory artifact is the **Decline-Attribution Record (DAR)** (method_note §6): a per-case, **routing-faithful** explanation of *why the model declined to answer* — the set of reliability gates that fired plus the signed margin to each threshold — read directly off the routing predicate the pipeline evaluated rather than approximated by a surrogate. It is **faithful to the routing, not to the diagnosis**: it asserts that the explanation matches the gate the pipeline actually used to route the case, not that the routing or the underlying class prediction is correct.

  Two of the paper's planned headline results live **only** in this audit layer, which is why deleting the explainability leg would cost the paper a headline, not merely a figure: (i) a **subgroup-attributable disparate-abstention finding** — whether abstention and routing rates concentrate on particular cohorts, attributable through the per-cohort routing-rate audit and the gate-firing breakdown of the DAR; and (ii) the **cohort-level shift-regime badge** (method_note §6.3), stating which correction regime (*pure-label-shift* vs *combined covariate+label*) is actually in force per target site. Both are audit-layer-only outputs: they are produced by the explanation machinery, not by the selective-risk estimator, and neither is a guarantee — the disparate-abstention finding is a *measured, reported* disparity and the badge is a *measured, checkable* regime label, not a control on either.

The fit is therefore not cosmetic: the paper's core deliverable — a measured, subgroup-auditable account of trustworthiness under realistic clinical shift, with no overclaimed guarantee — is precisely the kind of explainable, trustworthy clinical ML the collection solicits.
